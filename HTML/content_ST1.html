1.Introduction

    Oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface and play a vital role in maintaining global ecosystems. Marine life, including fish, corals, and microscopic organisms, supports biodiversity and provides food, oxygen, and economic resources for millions of people. Water is essential for all living beings, regulating climate, supporting habitats, and ensuring the survival of countless species.

2.Marine Biodiversity

    The ocean is home to a diverse range of organisms, from tiny plankton to massive whales. Coral reefs, often called the "rainforests of the sea," support thousands of marine species. Fish populations are crucial for both the marine food chain and human consumption, while deep-sea creatures thrive in extreme underwater environments, showcasing nature’s adaptability.

3.Threats to Marine Life

    Marine pollution, including plastic waste and oil spills, severely harms ocean ecosystems, poisoning marine animals and destroying habitats. Overfishing depletes fish populations, endangering species and disrupting the balance of the oceanic food chain. Human activities such as coastal development and bottom trawling lead to habitat destruction, threatening marine biodiversity. Climate change causes ocean acidification and rising sea temperatures, which lead to coral bleaching and negatively impact marine life

4.Human Impact on Marine Life

    Human activities have severely impacted marine life, causing pollution, overfishing, and climate change effects. Plastic waste, oil spills, and chemical runoff pollute oceans, harming marine animals and destroying habitats. Overfishing and illegal fishing methods deplete fish populations and disrupt the balance of marine ecosystems. Additionally, climate change leads to rising sea levels and ocean acidification, making it difficult for corals and shellfish to survive. These threats endanger biodiversity and the health of marine environments, highlighting the urgent need for conservation efforts to protect life below water.
